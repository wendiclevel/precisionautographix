What type of vehicle are you marking?

Select the option that best describes your vehicle.

Does your rig include a separate towed trailer or trailer unit?

This applies to any vehicle towing a separate unit — semi + trailer, truck + equipment trailer, pickup + chip trailer, tanker + pup, flatbed + rear trailer, etc. DOT placement rules differ for the towing unit vs. the trailer.

What is the vehicle's GVWR?

Gross Vehicle Weight Rating — found on the door jamb sticker. This determines which federal rules apply.

Trailer detected: Add your truck's GVWR + your trailer's GVWR to get the Gross Combination Weight Rating (GCWR). That combined number is what federal DOT rules measure — find the trailer's plate on the tongue or A-frame. If you are not sure, select the closest range and we'll note it.

How do you operate?

Select all that apply to your business.

Which state(s) do you operate in?

Select all states where your vehicles run. Even local operators have state-specific rules — FL, GA, and MS each have additional requirements for for-hire carriers.

What type of hazardous materials?

Select all that apply. This determines which placard class is required.

How is the hazmat transported?

This determines whether placard rules always apply or only above a quantity threshold — the two situations have different requirements under 49 CFR 172.504.
